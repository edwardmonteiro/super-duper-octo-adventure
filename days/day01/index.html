Day 01

Everything is made of pieces.

Everything you can see, touch, or smell is built from tiny pieces called atoms.

Oxygen (O) Hydrogen (H)

A water molecule — three atoms holding hands.

Pick up anything around you — a cup, a leaf, the air in front of your face. Zoom in. Then zoom in more, way past what your eyes can see, past what a microscope can show. Eventually you find atoms: tiny pieces from which everything is built.

Atoms are so small that about 5 million of them fit across the width of a single hair.

And here's the magic: there are only about 100 different kinds of atoms — we call them the elements. Every star, every cake, every cell in your body is built from these same ~100 pieces, combined in different ways.

When atoms "hold hands", they form a molecule. Two hydrogens plus one oxygen = one molecule of water. That's the picture above.
